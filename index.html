My Journey
The Story Behind the Work
"I remember early last year, right after my internship ended. I was sending out CVs almost every day. Reaching out to IT companies, even asking to volunteer just to get hands-on exposure on the networking side. Rejection after rejection. Some didn't even respond. At some point, you start questioning everything. But I told myself something simple: keep pushing. What's meant for me will find me along the way. That's when I stopped waiting and started building."

I set up my own home lab. Broke things, fixed them, learned how networks actually behave — not just in theory. No permission needed, no gatekeepers. It didn't remove the frustration, but it gave me control.

So if you're in that space where doors aren't opening — build your own. Learn anyway. Show up anyway.
